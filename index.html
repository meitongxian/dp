A CLOCK can be defined as an instrument for recording, measuring, and indicating time. The earliest clocks were based on observations of the movement of nature, such as the sundial that traced the sun's movement to mark the passing of a day. The clocks that we are most familiar with today, however, are products of technology, with their origins in the ropes and gears that struck the hours in medieval monasteries. The clock is no longer just a way of translating nature, but a way of rationalising nature.
        This collection of eleven clocks is a set of windows looking deeper into this devised structure that dictates our living and working life. They are a deliberately incomplete attempt to make sense of how this structure came to be, how it shapes our identities, how it informs our relationships with the world, how it has built the world we know, and how we can re-define our place in time.
        They are for everything.
